Open Source Technology Education

Abstract

As a current educator, still young in the field of Technology Education I continually wonder how is everyone else doing it? What are they teching? How are they teaching and what experiences have provided the best results relating to student progression. There are paid for networking clubs out there along with local organizations that also, include dues of some kind so this open-source format, such as open-source technology, would be built by those who wish to be involved, for those who truely care for continuous progression. My thinking is that if advertised well enough with a big enough viable repository to begin with the idea could catch on very quickly. The only fear is that of learning GitFlow and operating within GitHub which might be very foreign for most by the benefits outway the cons. For this thesis I decided to summarize that question into one line:

Could 'open-source' curriculum help synchronize planning between educators to better analyze student learning progression?

In order to help answer this question a repository was built and advertised to grow a collaborative effort in building a unified platform and/or repo of educational materials for current and upcoming technology educators. The idea is simple, "build it, and they will come." The foundation of this concept is the ever evolving, ever improving, ever adapting curriculum repository that not only could a single educator assist in, but anyone that felt interested in doing so.

In short:

Create an open-source curriculum to collaborate with other like-minded educators in an effort to synchronize and analyze student learning progression. This will be done by first providing the curriculum module in a sharable format (website powered by GitHub/gitflow) to then later on synchronize and collaborate student learning outcomes.
