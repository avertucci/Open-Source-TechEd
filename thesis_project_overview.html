The Current State of Curriculum Transfer

Currently, if you tried to investigate into the State of Curriculum or how it is progressing you would find very few answers to your question. Many publishings are simply broken down into a individualized field, race, or even just a particular educational institution. Rarely does anyone even ask 'What are the outcomes of the educational system'? What is the Curriculum-Development Process? These are all important questions that again, are often rarely asked or mentioned for any educaitonal purpose.
In a study done by Cornell University's George J. Posner, he mentions their are really only two (2) ways to actually study and approach Curriclum-Development Process's which is based on Structured-of-knowledge or Task-analysis. The structured knowledge-approach identifes ways for "knowledge producers to disocver potential curricular content". One of the key findings of this method is that the knowledge producers were found to, or were limted to investigating into the actual results of effect of their teachings or learning content. This is one key aspect, or fundamental that Open Source Curriclum hopefully addresses. As explained by George J. Posner, their was just not enough or little 'pilot testing' completed to actually determine whether the content was deemed teachable Many issues or findings between educators has been the transfer of knolwege or the actual approach to develop any such type of curricular. Many approaches involve various proving, 'pilot testing' or questionaire like approaches which are time consuming and somtimes, just in adequate based on the teaching envirnoment. Open Source Curriculum will be able to fill in that void, having the ability to address those questions on a wider scale, with a much larger sample size rather then just a few generations or classes. Take the case of WD-40 where a small working lab in San Diego took 40 attempts in order to get the was displacement formula working correct to create the product we know today. Any lesson plan or curriculum should take the same approach and not be stale or static but rather take on an idea of continuous improvement, your 40 plus or more tries in order to get it just right.

The Repo

The Open Souce TechEd repo is the heart of the project. It is the holding area of curriculums, lesson plans, assessment material, and educational learning material. If not familiar with Github well this is where we need to start. GitHub is a web-based Git repository hosting service. It offers all of the distributed revision control and source code management (SCM) functionality of Git as well as adding its own features. This is important to understand because every item that goes into the repository is version controlled adding a niche that no other project of this kind has within the educational domain of technology. Any overall folder or idicual item of the repo can be worked on indiviudally, versioned and then brought back into the MASTER branch as seen fit. One thing to take into account as this develops is that currently only myself will be in charge of the main MASTER branch which is the final, production version of all items. Users are able to branch off to create items of their own or improve current items but PULL REQUESTS can only be approved currently by myself until moderators are added.


Currently, the repository is housing a single 'Air Pullution' curriculum but is also currently in the process of exapnding to more curriculums by other educators and networking platforms that I have advertised to (Teach Engineering as one example). The Air Pollution curriculum in its final state will house over 50 lessson plans, each with supporting handouts and assessments for student progression. Think of this repository years from now with multitudes of learning documentation, also versioned in an unlimited storage envirnoment? The possabilties are endless with more people that it touches. For a user who does not want to contribute the repo can stil be easily downloaded as a zip folder and organized into lesson plans or curriculums to clearly indicated folder titles to look into that then provide support documentation. This will all also be identified in mark down readme files to provide simple clear instructions for new educators or those not familiar with GitHub.
